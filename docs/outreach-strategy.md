# Agency Outreach Strategy — Roadshow Creative

## Goal

Find 2–3 agencies like Matic Digital. The ideal outcome of first contact is a specific conversation about a current or upcoming project — not a generic intro call, not just being on a list.

---

## Target Agency Profile

What Matic Digital looks like — use this as the filter when building a target list:

- **Size:** 5–20 people
- **Focus:** Design-forward — strong creative/UX output, good visual sensibility
- **Gap:** Takes on web product or app work but lacks a senior technical lead in-house
- **Geography:** US-based (time zone matters for embedded work)
- **Signal words in their site copy:** "digital product," "web apps," "interactive," "experience design," "we build things" — anything that signals they're going beyond marketing sites

**Red flags (skip these):**
- Agencies that only do branding, print, or social
- Large agencies (50+ people) — they likely have internal technical leadership
- Agencies that heavily feature offshore or contract developer teams — they've already solved this differently

---

## Step 1 — Mine the Warm Network First

This is the highest-leverage starting point. One warm intro is worth ten cold messages.

**Do these first:**

1. **Go back to whoever introduced Matic Digital.** Ask directly: "Do you know any other agencies in a similar spot — design-forward, taking on product work, without a strong technical lead?" Most people are happy to make another intro if the first one worked out.

2. **Scan LinkedIn connections for agency people.** Look for: designers, creative directors, brand strategists, UX leads, agency founders. Anyone who works at or runs a small agency. Make a short list and check their agency's site before reaching out.

3. **Former colleagues who moved into agencies.** People you've worked with who are now at or running agencies are warm by default — they know your work.

**Goal from Step 1:** 3–5 warm leads before going cold.

---

## Step 2 — Build a Target List

Find 10–15 agencies that fit the profile. You'll reach out to them over time — not all at once.

**Where to find them:**

| Source | What to look for |
|--------|-----------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Search "digital agency" + city, or browse connections' employers |
| **Dribbble** | Agencies with strong design portfolios that mention "product" or "apps" in their bio |
| **Awwwards** | Award-winning agencies — high design bar, often lack technical depth |
| **Clutch** | Filter by "web development" + "UX/UI design," look at the smaller ones |
| **Agency directories** | Communo, Agency Spotter, DesignRush |

**For each agency, note:**
- Agency name + website
- Founder/principal name (for personalization)
- Something specific you noticed about their work (for the message)
- Do you have a mutual connection?

---

## Step 3 — Outreach Messages

### Warm Template (mutual connection)

Use this when someone can make an intro, or when you have a genuine shared connection with the recipient.

---

**Subject / Opening:** *(No subject if LinkedIn DM — just start the message)*

Hey [Name],

[Mutual] mentioned you might be worth connecting with — specifically around the technical side of product work your agency takes on.

I'm Seth. I run Roadshow Creative, a dev studio out of Oceanside, CA. I work with design-forward agencies as their embedded technical lead — handling architecture, engineering, and technical delivery on product builds while the agency stays focused on the client relationship.

Do you have anything coming up where that kind of support would be useful? Happy to have a quick conversation about what that looks like in practice.

— Seth

---

### Cold Template (no connection)

Use this when reaching out without a mutual. Personalize the [specific observation] for every single agency — one generic line that shows you actually looked at their work. This is what separates it from spam.

---

**Subject / Opening:** *(LinkedIn connection request or DM)*

Hey [Name],

I came across [Agency Name] — [specific observation about their work, e.g., "the Acme project you posted last month is exactly the kind of product work I love seeing agencies take on"].

I'm Seth, founder of Roadshow Creative. I specialize in embedding with design-forward agencies as a technical lead on product builds — the person who owns the architecture and technical delivery so the agency can stay focused on the client.

If you have current or upcoming work where that kind of support would be useful, I'd love to have a quick conversation.

— Seth
roadshowcreative.com

---

**Rules for the cold message:**
- Personalize [specific observation] every time — no exceptions. If you can't find something genuine to say about their work, skip them.
- Keep it short — under 100 words if possible. The goal is a reply, not a pitch deck.
- Don't attach anything, don't link to a portfolio deck. The site link is enough.
- Don't ask for a "30-minute call" in the first message — just ask if there's something to talk about.

---

## Step 4 — Follow-Up Rule

**One follow-up. That's it.**

If no response after one week, send one short follow-up:

> "Hey [Name] — just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Happy to connect if the timing is right."

If still no response, move on. No chasing. The goal is to be remembered positively, not to grind someone into a meeting.
