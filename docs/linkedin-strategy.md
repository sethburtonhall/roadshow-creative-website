# LinkedIn Strategy — Roadshow Creative

## Goal

Be findable and credible to agency founders who need an embedded technical lead. Post consistently enough to stay visible — not so much that it becomes a burden.

**Target:** 1–2 posts per week. Quality over volume.

## Three Content Pillars

Rotate between these. Don't force a schedule — just know which bucket a post idea falls into and pull from whichever feels right that week.

---

### Pillar 1 — Building Village in Public

The easiest content because it's already happening. Product decisions, progress updates, lessons learned. Shows product thinking, transparency, and that you ship.

**Example angles:**
- A product decision you made and why
- Something that didn't work and what you learned
- A milestone (waitlist launch, first users, first paid signup)
- A technical choice made for Village and the reasoning behind it

---

### Pillar 2 — Technical Takes

Short opinions on stack choices, architecture, modern web dev. Not tutorials — just takes. This is what proves to an agency founder that you know what you're doing.

**Example angles:**
- Why you chose X over Y
- A pattern you've stopped using (and what replaced it)
- A tool or library that changed how you think
- A common mistake you see in production codebases

---

### Pillar 3 — Agency + Engineering Observations

Content from the intersection of design and engineering is rare and highly relevant to your ICP. Agency founders recognize it immediately because it describes their world.

**Example angles:**
- The gap between design and engineering in agency projects
- What makes a good technical partner (vs. a contractor)
- Why agencies lose technical work (it's not the lack of developers)
- What "owning the technical delivery" actually looks like day-to-day

---

## Format Rules

- **First line is everything.** It's all they see before "see more." Make it a hook, a claim, or a surprising observation.
- **Short paragraphs.** 2–3 sentences max per block. White space is your friend.
- **1–2 hashtags or none.** No spam.
- **End with a soft question** occasionally — it invites comments without begging for engagement.
- **Reposts with 2–3 lines of commentary count** as a post. Use them.

---

## What Not To Do

- Don't post 5 times in a week then disappear for a month — consistency beats volume
- Don't write for the algorithm — write for the one agency founder reading it
- Don't lead with your credentials — lead with an observation, a problem, or a story
- Don't announce things without a point of view — "I launched X" is less interesting than "I launched X and here's what surprised me"
